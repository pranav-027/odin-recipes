Banana Oat Pancake

Wholesome and satisfying, these pancakes are a nutritious twist on a classic favorite. Made with blended oats, ripe bananas, and eggs, these hearty pancakes are gluten-free and packed with fiber. Enjoy these fluffy delights topped with fresh fruits or a touch of maple syrup for a guilt-free breakfast treat.

Ingredients

  • 1 ripe banana
  • 1 cup rolled oats (gluten-free if needed)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of cinnamon (optional)
  • Oil or butter for greasing the pan
  • Fresh fruits, yogurt, or maple syrup for serving (optional)

Steps to Make:

  1. In a blender or food processor, blend the rolled oats until they reach a flour-like consistency.
  2. Add the ripe banana, eggs, baking powder, vanilla extract, and a pinch of cinnamon (if using) to the blender. Blend until the batter is smooth and well combined.
  3. Let the batter rest for a few minutes to thicken slightly.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil or butter.
  5. Pour small amounts of batter onto the skillet to form pancakes.
  6. Cook for about 2-3 minutes on one side until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until golden brown.
  7. Repeat the process with the remaining batter.
  8. Serve the banana oat pancakes warm, optionally topped with fresh fruits, a dollop of yogurt, or a drizzle of maple syrup for added flavor.
