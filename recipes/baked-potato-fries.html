Baked Potato Fries

Crispy on the outside and fluffy on the inside, these Baked Potato Fries are seasoned with paprika, garlic, and onion powder for a flavorful twist on a classic snack or side dish—perfectly satisfying without the need for frying. Enjoy these healthier homemade fries as a delicious guilt-free treat!

Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes, scrubbed and dried
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Optional: Fresh parsley for garnish

Steps to Make:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cut the scrubbed and dried potatoes into even-sized sticks to create fries.
  3. In a large bowl, toss the potato sticks with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper until evenly coated.
  4. Spread the seasoned potato fries in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet, ensuring they are not overcrowded.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes, flipping the fries halfway through the baking time for even cooking.
  6. Once the fries are golden brown and crispy, remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly.
  7. Optionally, garnish with fresh parsley for added flavor and presentation.
  8. Serve the baked potato fries hot as a healthier alternative to traditional fried potatoes, perfect as a snack or side dish.
